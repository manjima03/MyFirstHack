Day 13 of 90 with @MyFirstHack.

Today I opened the cookie jar of a site I use every day. I'd clicked through a thousand cookie banners without ever looking at what was on the other side.

I got to edit a session cookie and test out all the stuff I had studied but did not get to try out. This actually helped me get to know cookies as a part of the web and not just another topic to study.

Cookies are not the dystopian thing the press makes them out to be. They're a small text file that lets the web remember who you are between page loads. They're also one of the most stolen things on the internet, because if an attacker has your cookie, the website thinks they are you — even if you have multi-factor authentication switched on.

Two weeks in. The web is starting to feel less like magic and more like machinery.
