Day 14 of 90 with @MyFirstHack.

I've had a Wi-Fi router for over a decade. Today was the first time I tried to log into the admin panel and audit it the way a security analyst would.

I was surprised to find that the password had already been changed! Kudos to whoever did that. Also found a vulnerability on the admin login page, which was unexpected. 

A home Wi-Fi router is one device doing about five different security-relevant jobs at once. Most of us change the Wi-Fi password and never touch any of the rest. Default admin passwords. Firmware from years ago. Smart bulbs sharing a network with the laptop you do your taxes on.
