Day 11 of 90 with @MyFirstHack.

I just opened the developer tools on a website I use every single day, and watched it make over 86 separate network requests in the time it took to load.

It surprised me that so many requests and responses are handled by the system in such a short amount of time. Eventhough I have seen pages loading a million times, actually observing the process made me want to get depper into the topic. 

A week ago I would have called that page "loaded." Today I can see it's still talking to a dozen different servers, running scripts from companies I never heard of, and that every one of those connections is a place security has to hold up.

The web is way bigger than what you see.
