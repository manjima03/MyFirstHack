Day 12 of 90 with @MyFirstHack.

I've looked at a padlock in my browser thousands of times. Today was the first time I actually opened one up.

The more I get to know of past attacks and the more attack surfaces I get to learn about, it amazes me how much work goes on in securing these systems. The DigiNotar story and how it was discovered truly blew my mind.

The padlock proves the connection is encrypted. It does not prove the site is who you think it is, and it does not prove the site is safe. About 71% of malware now arrives over HTTPS. The icon attackers fear most is the same icon they hide behind.

Two weeks in. Starting to see how the layers of the web actually fit together.
